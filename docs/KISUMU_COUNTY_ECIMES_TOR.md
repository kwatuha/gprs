## 1. BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT

### 1.1 Introduction
The Electronic County Integrated Management and Evaluation System (ECIMES) is a comprehensive web-based platform designed to enable Kisumu County Government to manage, track, monitor, and report on infrastructure and development projects transparently and efficiently. The system facilitates real-time project monitoring, budget management, stakeholder collaboration, and public engagement.

ECIMES represents a strategic initiative aligned with the Kenya Devolution Support Programme Phase II (KDSP II) requirements, designed to transform the county's digital infrastructure for managing public investments. The system addresses critical gaps in transparency, accountability, and public participation in county governance, enabling evidence-based decision-making and strengthening citizen participation in development monitoring.

### 1.2 Project Justification

#### 1.2.1 Alignment with National and County Priorities
- **KDSP II Compliance**: ECIMES is designed to fully integrate all aspects of KDSP II requirements into a unified digital platform, ensuring compliance with national standards for managing public investments through the Project Information Management System (PIMS).
- **Devolution Objectives**: The system supports the devolution agenda by enhancing county capacity for transparent and accountable project management, aligning with constitutional requirements for public participation and accountability.
- **Vision 2030 Alignment**: ECIMES contributes to Kenya Vision 2030 goals by promoting efficient public service delivery, transparency, and citizen engagement in governance.

#### 1.2.2 Current Challenges and Gaps
Despite the deployment of ECIMES, Kisumu County faces several operational limitations that prevent optimal system utilization:

1. **Limited Public Visibility**: Insufficient transparency in government operations prevents citizens from effectively monitoring public investments and holding officials accountable. The public dashboard and feedback mechanisms require proper configuration and promotion.

2. **Incomplete Process Integration**: Existing county workflows are not fully aligned with ECIMES capabilities, creating inefficiencies and reducing the system's effectiveness. Manual processes continue to run parallel to the system rather than being integrated.

3. **Weak Citizen Feedback Mechanisms**: Existing channels for public input are fragmented, making it difficult to capture and act upon community concerns systematically. The system's feedback and grievance management features require proper setup and staff training.

4. **Poor Inter-departmental Coordination**: Siloed information systems and lack of standardized processes hinder collaboration and create inefficiencies in project planning and implementation.

5. **Insufficient Staff Capacity**: County staff lack comprehensive training on system utilization, process workflows, and best practices, limiting the system's effectiveness.

6. **Incomplete Data Migration**: Historical project data and budget information may not be fully migrated or validated, affecting system completeness and accuracy.

7. **Limited Process Documentation**: Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for system use are not fully developed, leading to inconsistent practices across departments.

#### 1.2.3 Expected Benefits of Effective System Utilization
When properly implemented and utilized, ECIMES will deliver significant improvements across multiple dimensions:

- **Enhanced Transparency**: Real-time public access to comprehensive project information, including budgets, timelines, contractors, and progress updates, building public trust and accountability.

- **Improved Accountability**: Complete audit trails, automated compliance checks, and performance metrics that enable effective oversight and evaluation by both internal and external stakeholders.

- **Increased Citizen Engagement**: Direct feedback channels, grievance mechanisms, and participatory monitoring tools that empower communities to participate meaningfully in development processes.

- **Operational Efficiency**: Automated workflows, standardized templates, and integrated systems that reduce administrative burden and accelerate project delivery.

- **Data-Driven Decision Making**: Advanced analytics, visualization tools, and reporting capabilities that support evidence-based planning and resource allocation.

- **Enhanced Compliance**: Built-in mechanisms to ensure adherence to KDSP II standards, national regulations, and county policies.

- **Optimized Resource Allocation**: Better visibility into project performance and resource utilization, enabling more strategic investment decisions.

### 1.3 System Overview
ECIMES is a multi-module system that includes:
- **Project Management Module**: Complete lifecycle management of county projects from identification through closure
- **Budget Management System**: Budget planning, approval workflows, and financial tracking with departmental and county-wide budget containers
- **Strategic Planning Module**: CIDP integration, programs, and sub-programs management
- **Public Dashboard**: Transparent public access to project information with interactive filtering and geospatial visualization
- **Reporting and Analytics**: Comprehensive reporting and data visualization with export capabilities
- **User Management**: Role-based access control and privilege management
- **Document Management**: Project documents, attachments, and approvals
- **Feedback System**: Citizen engagement, feedback mechanisms, and grievance management
- **Monitoring System**: Field inspection records, progress tracking, and milestone management
- **Contractor Management**: Contractor database and project-contractor linkages

### 1.4 Current Status
The ECIMES system (v5.0) has been deployed and is operational. The system infrastructure is in place, and basic functionality is available. However, for optimal utilization and to realize the full benefits of the system, Kisumu County Government requires external support to:

1. **Develop and Document Processes**: Create comprehensive Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) that align county workflows with system capabilities
2. **Build Internal Capacity**: Provide comprehensive training to county staff at all levels
3. **Establish Data Governance**: Implement policies and procedures for data quality, security, and integrity
4. **Optimize System Configuration**: Ensure all system features are properly configured and utilized
5. **Facilitate Change Management**: Support the transition from manual to digital processes

This Terms of Reference (TOR) is designed to guide the procurement of consulting services from a qualified firm that will assist Kisumu County in achieving these objectives.

### 3.7 KDSP II / CIMES Enhancement Alignment (Key Requirements Extracted from `RFP.docx`)

Kisumu County’s ECIMES operationalization should be aligned to the KDSP II Project Information Management System (PIMS) expectations. The County previously used a formal RFP/TOR for CIMES enhancement (`/home/dev/dev/imes_working/v5/budgets/RFP.docx`, **Issue Date:** 14 July 2025, **Close Date:** 29 July 2025, **Ref:** CGK/PSCA&PD/RFP/KDSP/2025-2026/002). The requirements below should be adopted as **county operational standards** and, where gaps exist, as **future enhancement requirements**.

#### 3.7.1 Four Major Capability Components (KDSP II-aligned)
- **Integrated Project Lifecycle Management System (IPLMS)**: end-to-end project management from concept to closure, including technical/financial/economic viability and risk assessments.
- **Public Engagement Portal (PEP)**: transparency interface for public feedback, structured participation, and formal grievance management.
- **Mobile-Enabled Project Stock-Taking Tools (MPSTT)**: a field-ready tool (PWA) for data capture, validation, and near real-time synchronization.
- **User Training & Capacity Building**: a sustainable ownership model anchored on Training of Trainers (ToT).

#### 3.7.2 Minimum Deliverables (Adopt as County Acceptance Criteria)
- **Fully developed / configured and integrated platform** covering IPLMS + PEP + MPSTT capabilities (whether within ECIMES configuration or via phased enhancement).
- **System design and architecture documentation** (including integration and data-flow documentation).
- **Training of Trainers (ToT) program** and full training curriculum delivered (and embedded in county onboarding).
- **Data migration plan** and verified transfer of existing project data (including validation and reconciliation approach).
- **Comprehensive user and administrator manuals** (kept current as the system evolves).
- **Minimum 6-month post-deployment support & maintenance plan** (even for internal releases, the County should define an equivalent internal support plan).

#### 3.7.3 Project Lifecycle Coverage (Operational Requirements)
To achieve consistent monitoring and evaluation, ECIMES use should cover (at minimum) the following lifecycle stages and records:
- **Project identification & concept planning**: standardized concept notes; needs assessment; stakeholder identification; preliminary budgeting.
- **Pre-appraisal / pre-feasibility**: workflows and documentation; environmental/social screening; hazard & risk checklist capture.
- **Feasibility & appraisal**: technical, financial, economic, social, and environmental assessments; risk mitigation; decision criteria.
- **Selection for budgeting**: prioritization matrices/scoring; approval routing; linkage to budget containers/items.
- **Implementation**: work planning; procurement tracking; contract management; milestone/progress tracking; budget execution monitoring; change management (variations).
- **Closure & sustainability**: completion verification; handover; lessons learned; asset registration; sustainability assessment.

#### 3.7.4 Public Participation and Grievance Handling (Operational Requirements)
- **Feedback and grievances** must be logged, categorized, assigned, and tracked to closure with response timelines.
- **Public-facing project information** should be kept up to date (budget summaries, timelines, progress, and key documents suitable for publication).

### 3.9 Hands-On Operational Guide: Practical Tasks and Workflows

This section provides step-by-step guidance for key operational tasks that county staff must perform regularly in ECIMES. These hands-on examples should be incorporated into training programs and standard operating procedures.

#### 3.9.1 Project Inspection and Monitoring

**Task 3.9.1.1: Conducting Field Project Inspection**

**Purpose**: Record on-site observations, challenges, and recommendations for project monitoring.

**Step-by-Step Process**:

1. **Access Project Details**:
   - Navigate to: Projects → Select project → Project Details page
   - Or use direct URL: `/impes/projects/{projectId}`

2. **Navigate to Monitoring Section**:
   - Click on "Monitoring" tab in project details
   - Review existing monitoring records if any

3. **Create New Monitoring Record**:
   - Click "Add Monitoring Record" button
   - Fill in the monitoring form:
     - **Observation Date**: Date of field visit
     - **Comment/Observation**: Detailed description of what was observed
     - **Recommendations**: Specific actions recommended
     - **Challenges**: Issues or obstacles identified
     - **Warning Level**: Select from dropdown (None, Low, Medium, High, Critical)
     - **Is Routine Observation**: Check if this is a scheduled inspection

4. **Attach Supporting Documents** (if applicable):
   - Upload photos from field visit
   - Attach inspection reports
   - Link to related documents

5. **Submit and Save**:
   - Click "Save" to create the monitoring record
   - System automatically timestamps and assigns to current user

**Best Practices**:
- Conduct inspections monthly for active projects
- Use warning levels to flag critical issues requiring immediate attention
- Link monitoring records to project milestones
- Include photos for visual documentation
- Follow up on recommendations in subsequent inspections

**System Features Used**:
- API Endpoint: `POST /api/projects/{projectId}/monitoring`
- Frontend Component: Project Monitoring section in Project Details page
- Database Table: `kemri_project_monitoring_records`

#### 3.9.2 Budget Management: Creating Departmental Budgets

**Task 3.9.2.1: Creating a Departmental Budget Container**

**Purpose**: Set up a budget container for a specific department's projects for a financial year.

**Step-by-Step Process**:

1. **Access Budget Management**:
   - Navigate to: Budget Management → Budget Containers
   - Or use direct URL: `/impes/budgets`

2. **Create New Budget Container**:
   - Click "Create Budget Container" or "New Budget" button
   - Fill in the budget form:
     - **Budget Name**: e.g., "2025/2026 - Agriculture Department Budget"
     - **Financial Year**: Select from dropdown (must be pre-configured)
     - **Department**: Select the department (required)
     - **Description**: Optional detailed description
     - **Budget Type**: Select (Draft, Pending, Approved)
     - **Requires Approval for Changes**: Check if changes need approval after budget is approved

3. **Save Budget Container**:
   - Click "Save" to create the container
   - System assigns a unique `budgetId`

4. **Add Budget Items (Projects)**:
   - Open the created budget container
   - Click "Add Item" or "Import Items"
   - **Option A - Manual Entry**:
     - Click "Add Item" button
     - Enter project name, amount, location (ward/subcounty)
     - Link to existing project or create new project
   - **Option B - Bulk Import**:
     - Click "Import Budget Items"
     - Download template: `/api/budgets/template`
     - Fill template with project names, amounts, locations
     - Upload file: System will match projects and create/update as needed

5. **Review and Validate**:
   - Check total budget amount matches department allocation
   - Verify all projects are correctly linked
   - Ensure location data is accurate

6. **Submit for Approval** (if required):
   - Change status to "Pending"
   - Submit to approver
   - Track approval status

**Best Practices**:
- Create budget containers at the start of each financial year
- Use consistent naming convention: "{FY} - {Department} Budget"
- Import items in bulk using Excel template for efficiency
- Validate department assignments before finalizing
- Link all budget items to actual projects in the system

**System Features Used**:
- API Endpoint: `POST /api/budgets/containers`
- Import Endpoint: `POST /api/budgets/import-data`
- Frontend Page: Budget Management Page

#### 3.9.3 Budget Management: Creating Master/County Budgets

**Task 3.9.3.1: Creating a Combined/County-Wide Budget**

**Purpose**: Aggregate multiple departmental budgets into a master county budget for oversight and reporting.

**Step-by-Step Process**:

1. **Create Master Budget Container**:
   - Navigate to: Budget Management → Budget Containers
   - Click "Create Budget Container"
   - Fill in:
     - **Budget Name**: e.g., "Kisumu County 2025/2026 Master Budget"
     - **Financial Year**: Select financial year
     - **Department**: Leave NULL or select "County-Wide"
     - **Description**: "Combined budget for all departments"
     - **Budget Type**: "Combined" or "Master"

2. **Add Departmental Budgets as Containers**:
   - Open the master budget
   - Navigate to "Combined Budgets" section
   - Click "Add Container to Combined Budget"
   - Select existing departmental budget containers to include
   - System automatically calculates total from all containers

3. **Review Combined Totals**:
   - View aggregated totals across all departments
   - Check budget distribution by department
   - Verify alignment with county budget allocation

4. **Generate Master Budget Report**:
   - Use reporting features to generate:
     - Budget summary by department
     - Project distribution across departments
     - Location-based budget breakdown
     - Financial year overview

**Alternative Approach - Direct County Budget Creation**:

1. **Create County Budget Container**:
   - Same as departmental budget but without specific department assignment
   - Or assign to "County Administration" department

2. **Import All County Projects**:
   - Use comprehensive import template
   - Include projects from all departments
   - System will maintain department linkages

**Best Practices**:
- Create master budget after all departmental budgets are finalized
- Use combined budget feature for aggregation
- Regularly update master budget as departmental budgets change
- Generate monthly/quarterly reports from master budget

**System Features Used**:
- API Endpoint: `POST /api/budgets/containers/combined`
- Combined Budget Endpoint: `GET /api/budgets/containers/{budgetId}/combined`
- Frontend: Budget Management Page with Combined Budget view
